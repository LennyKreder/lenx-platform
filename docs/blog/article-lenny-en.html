I make things— that’s the tl;dr. I write code, music, and texts. I’m a mom, a software developer. I design planners. I think a lot (sometimes too much). And I identify problems and figure out the solutions.

I started Layouts by Lenny because I could never find a planner that worked how I wanted it to. Of course, there’s already an abundance of planners out there. Why put in all that work to create a new one? Because the vast majority of them are in English. And finding a Dutch one seems impossible. In a catalogue of blinding colors, feelgood quotes, a thousand sticker packs and infinite templates no one actually uses— it’s time for some simplicity.

Filofax has been my go-to planner for years, and it’s still one I hold a lot of love for. It was only two years ago that I found out my creative scatterbrain was, in part, made possible by ADHD. Aha! That’s why I’ve always had to write things down so I don’t forget. Now it makes sense. For years I’ve been printing out my own Filofax layouts, because nothing else suits my out-of-the-box way of planning.

In recent years I’ve discovered the world of digital planning on the Ipad. Amazing. But also very overwhelming. The best part of digital planning for me is that you can do it on all kinds of devices. Adding entries to my schedule is easiest on a device with a pen like the iPad or the reMarkable paper Pro (and the Move). And you can easily view them on any screen, like your phone which is always accessible.

So, that’s how I started programming (in Python, for my fellow nerds) to create my own digital planner for the iPad. At first, only a simple week layout and daily overview. Just playing around and testing wat works. At this point, my trash folder on Noteshelf is overflowing with all my discarded layouts and different versions. But finally, with the help of my daughter lending a scrutinizing eye, I’ve managed to create a very complete, but still easy to digest planner. Available in many different themes, including several optional templates. I hope there’s something there for you.

If you have any feedback or suggestions, I’d love to hear from you!
