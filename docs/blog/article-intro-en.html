I make things. That's kind of the short version. I write code, I write music, I write words. I'm a mom. I build software. I design planners. I overthink things and then build something for it.

If that sounds like a lot of different directions — it is. But they all come from the same place. I like creating things that didn't exist before. Whether that's a piece of music, a piece of software, or a planner page that finally feels right. The medium changes, the itch doesn't.

Layouts by Lenny started because I couldn't find a planner that felt right. They were either too busy, too rigid, or too... not in Dutch. That last one surprised me. I went looking for a good digital planner in Dutch and the options were basically nonexistent. Plenty of English ones. Plenty of beautiful, feature-packed, overwhelmingly detailed English ones. But if you wanted something clean, simple, and in your own language? Good luck.

So I made my own. First just for me. A simple weekly layout, some daily pages, a monthly overview. Nothing fancy. But it worked. It actually worked. I opened it every day, which was more than I could say for any planner I'd bought before. And then I kept making it better. Tweaking margins, adjusting font sizes, adding more layout options. Before I knew it, I had over ninety templates and a system that did exactly what I needed.

And then I thought, maybe someone else is looking for this too. Maybe there's another person out there who just wants a clean planner in Dutch without motivational quotes and moon phase trackers. So here we are.

I'm a developer by trade, which means I automate everything I can. The planners are fully generated with code — every layout, every link, every tab. No dragging boxes around in design software. No copy-pasting pages one by one — because that's how mistakes happen. One wrong link, one broken tab, and you're stuck with a planner that doesn't work for the rest of the year. By generating everything, I know that every page, every link, and every reference is exactly right. It's also why I can offer so many options. Week starting on Monday or Sunday, 24-hour or AM/PM time format, different calendar layouts — these are all choices you make when you download. Your planner, your preferences.

This is also why I can offer so many themes. Fifteen at last count, and growing. Light and dark versions, different color palettes, even a terminal theme inspired by the code editor I stare at all day. Because everything is code-generated, every theme gets the same attention to detail. Same precise spacing, same clean navigation, same tested layouts. Just a different palette. That consistency is hard to get right by hand.

Outside of planners, my life is a collection of obsessions that rotate in and out. I play music. I write. I have a Filofax filled with inserts I print myself and fountain pens that cost more than they should. I carry my reMarkable Move from room to room — you know, just in case I have something important to write down. Sometimes I don't. But it HAS to be with me when I do.

The planners started as a side project and kind of took over. Which I'm fine with. There's something deeply satisfying about building a product you actually use every single day. I'm not designing planners in theory. I'm designing the planner I reach for at 7am and again at 10pm. If something annoys me, I fix it. If I wish a feature existed, I build it. That's the luxury of being both the maker and the user.

I think the thing that drives all of this — the code, the music, the planners, the writing — is a need for things to feel right. Not perfect. Perfect is a trap. But right. The right amount of space on a page. The right weight of lines. The right balance between structure and freedom. When something feels right, you stop thinking about it and start using it. That's what I'm going for.

So that's me. A developer who makes planners. A musician who writes code. A mom who carries a tablet from room to room just in case inspiration strikes between the kitchen and the couch. Welcome to Layouts by Lenny. I hope something here feels right for you too.
